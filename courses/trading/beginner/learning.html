Introduction to Markets

Lesson 1

Financial markets are where traders buy and sell assets like Stocks, Forex (Currencies), and Cryptocurrencies.

The goal is simple: Buy Low, Sell High (Long) or Sell High, Buy Low (Short). Markets are driven by Supply (Sellers) and Demand (Buyers).

Key Concept: Bull vs. Bear

Bull Market: Prices are rising. Optimism is high.
Bear Market: Prices are falling. Fear is high.

Japanese Candlesticks

Lesson 2

Candlesticks are the language of price action. They tell you what happened in a specific time period.

Each candle has a Body (Open to Close range) and Wicks (High and Low points).

Bullish (Green)
Bearish (Red)

Green: Close > Open | Red: Close < Open

Market Trends

Lesson 3

"The Trend is your Friend." Identifying the market direction is crucial for success.

  • Uptrend: Higher Highs (HH) and Higher Lows (HL).
  • Downtrend: Lower Highs (LH) and Lower Lows (LL).
  • Sideways (Range): Price moves between a high and low bound.

Support & Resistance

Lesson 4

These are psychological price levels where price often reacts.

Support (Floor): A level where price stops falling and bounces up. Buyers step in here.
Resistance (Ceiling): A level where price stops rising and rejects down. Sellers step in here.

When Resistance is broken, it often becomes new Support. This is called a Flip.

Risk Management

Lesson 5

The #1 rule of trading: Protect your Capital.

Never risk more than 1-2% of your account on a single trade. Use a Stop Loss to limit losses significantly.

Simple Risk Example

Account Size: $1,000

Risk per Trade: 2%

Max Loss Allowed: $20
