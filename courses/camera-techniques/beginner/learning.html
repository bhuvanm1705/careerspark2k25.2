The Camera Eye

Lesson 1

Photography is painting with light. To master it, you need to move beyond "Auto" mode and understand how your camera sees the world.

The foundation of every photo is the Exposure Triangle: Aperture, Shutter Speed, and ISO. Balancing these three elements gives you total creative control.

Aperture (f-stop)

Lesson 2

Aperture controls the amount of light entering the lens and the Depth of Field (blur).

f/1.8
Wide Open
Blurred Background
(Portrait)

f/16
Closed Down
Everything Sharp
(Landscape)

Remember: Low f-number = More Light + Blurry Background.

Shutter Speed

Lesson 3

Shutter Speed controls the duration of light exposure and Motion Blur.

  • Fast Shutter (1/1000s): Freezes action (Sports, Birds).
  • Slow Shutter (1/30s or longer): Blurs motion (Waterfalls, light trails). Needs a tripod!

ISO Sensitivity

Lesson 4

ISO measures the sensor's sensitivity to light.

Low ISO (100): Cleanest image, best for bright daylight.
High ISO (3200+): Allows shooting in dark, but introduces Noise (Grain).

Always keep ISO as low as possible to maintain image quality.

Composition

Lesson 5

How you arrange elements in the frame makes the difference between a snapshot and Art.

The Rule of Thirds

Imagine a 3x3 grid over your image. Place your subject on the intersections, not dead center.
